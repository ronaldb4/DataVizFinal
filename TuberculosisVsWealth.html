Tuberculosis and Wealth

The novelist Charles Dickens wrote of tuberculosis in 1838 as “a dread disease” which “medicine never cured,” and which “wealth never warded off.” An illness “in which death and life are so strangely blended, that death takes the glow and hue of life, and life the gaunt and grisly form of death.” Health officials once predicted tuberculosis would be eradicated by 1915. They were wrong. Yet in the United States, where tuberculosis has been on the decline since 1992, there’s a vague perception that it is a historic disease—long since cured and largely forgotten.

It may have been the case when Charles Dickens was alive that "wealth never warded off" tuberculosis, but today there is a clear correlation between income of a society and its incidence of tuberculosis.

A regression line makes this clear and shows that most countries follow this relationship.

But when we examine the data more closely, we can see that there are countries that deviate significantly from this general trend.

These outliers have an interesting story to tell.

Examining these outliers we can see that there can be considerable variability in TB incidence for countries with similar levels of income.

Dominica and Gabon have a very similar GDP per Capita, but experience very different levels of TB incidence.

Countries with very different income levels can experience similar levels of TB incidence.

Burkina Faso and Singapore experience very similar levels of TB incidence, despite Burkina Faso having 1% of the income of Singapore.

Examining the data in the context of geography may provide additional insight into what drives a countries TB Incidence.

Isolating the countries of East Asia and the Pacific, we can see that the general relationship between income and TB incidence holds, but that there is a generally higher incidence than the global norm.

Isolating the countries of Sub-Saharan Africa the global relationship between income and TB incidence weakens considerably. This may indicate that location plays a significant role.
